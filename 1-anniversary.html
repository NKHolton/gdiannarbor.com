Girl Develop It Ann Arbor Celebrates One Year of Empowering Women in Technology with Downtown Fundraiser

ANN ARBOR, May 11, 2015 -- On Thursday, June 4th, Girl Develop It Ann Arbor will celebrate its first anniversary at The Forge 4.0, Pillar’s brand new, downtown office space.

One year ago, two women set out to establish an Ann Arbor chapter of the national non-profit, Girl Develop It (GDI). Co-founders Julie Cameron and Ronda Bergman envisioned giving local women opportunities to learn web and software development in an affordable and judgment-free environment.

Over the past year, the Ann Arbor chapter has held over 50 events, including educational workshops, informational talks, happy hours, and other social meet ups. Today, they now have over 900 members, 25 instructors and 40 volunteers.

“The involvement and growth we’ve seen in the Ann Arbor GDI community has completely exceeded all of our expectations and we couldn’t be prouder,” states Cameron.

To celebrate their first anniversary, GDI Ann Arbor is hosting a celebratory fundraiser to highlight the lives they have positively impacted and to look ahead at the chapter’s exciting future. Guests will hear brief remarks from some incredible students, instructors and volunteers whose lives have been impacted by their involvement with GDI, get to mingle with friends and community members who've supported the growth and success of GDI throughout the year, enjoy delicious food and drinks - courtesy of Pillar, and try their luck at winning a raffle prize or two from their gracious donors.

GDI Ann Arbor has invited over 40 local, hiring companies to the celebration. Giving guests plenty of opportunities for networking and engagement with the Ann Arbor tech community.

Tickets are on sale now at http://bit.ly/gdia2-anniversary. Early-bird tickets are available until Friday, May 15th. All proceeds from the event will go toward Girl Develop It programming and scholarships to help women, including underrepresented and unemployed women, take GDI Ann Arbor classes to further their careers.


ABOUT GIRL DEVELOP IT

Girl Develop It is a nonprofit organization that provides affordable programs for adult women interested in learning web and software development in a judgment-free environment. “Girl, Develop It!” began as a call to action for women, by women who were tired of the low representation of women in tech, and wanted to make a difference. The organization was founded in New York City in 2010 by Vanessa Hurst and Sara Chipps. Today, Girl Develop It chapters are building strong, empowered communities in 46 cities, representing 35,000+ members nationwide who are changing their lives through learning to code with us. http://girldevelopit.com
